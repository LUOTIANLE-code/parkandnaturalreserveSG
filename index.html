Park and Natural Reserves in Singapore

Singapore is known as the “Garden City”, with numerous parks and nature reserves that blend modern urban and natural landscapes in harmony. These green spaces provide opportunities for tourists and citizens to relax, hike, and explore biodiversity, demonstrating Singapore's commitment to environmental protection.

The storymap displays the locations of parks and nature reserves in Singapore, and displays them in color by area size. It introduces the distribution of park facilities and subway accessibility from the perspective of visitors, and highlights the largest nature reserve,which is Bukit Timah Natural Reserve.

Through the crowdsourced map, users can report the location, time, and species of wildlife discoveries, and view their distribution on a map.

By combining park facilities and accessibility data, the effectiveness of Singapore's green system in balancing human activities and wildlife conservation can be visually demonstrated.
